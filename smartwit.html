The SmartWit Project

Initial Deployment: November 2017
Latest Update: October 2019
Project Type: Website & Entrepreneurship Cell

Description

The Smart-Wit Project emerged as a pioneering initiative within my college, initially conceived as an Entrepreneurship Cell (E-Cell) and later evolving into a prominent activity group dedicated to fostering entrepreneurial spirit and skill development among students. Spearheading the establishment of the college's inaugural E-Cell, my team and I collaborated closely with faculty mentors to simulate the intricacies of launching in-campus companies, navigating through realistic yet simulated complexities and paperwork associated with entrepreneurial ventures. Under the umbrella of The Smartwit Project, a diverse array of activities were organized, including personal interviews, mock drills, group discussions, leadership exercises, team-building initiatives, Physical games like “Chakravyuh” and annual orientation programs for incoming students. Additionally, we facilitated workshops covering cutting-edge technologies that were not covered in our syllabus such as Game Development, Internet of Things using Raspberry Pi, and Machine Learning, ensuring students remained abreast of the latest advancements in their respective fields. Notably, The Smartwit Project also initiated the college's first student-based magazine, "Plethora", providing a platform for creative expression and intellectual discourse within the campus community. Through strategic initiatives and collaborative efforts, The Smartwit Project experienced exponential growth, expanding to encompass 25 field members at its peak and attracting over 120 registered members campus-wide. Leveraging our platforms, we mentored and supported 12 budding entrepreneurs in establishing their own college-based ventures, while also providing invaluable assistance to numerous students through our immersive mock drills and workshops.
